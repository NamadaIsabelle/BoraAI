# BoraCare AI – Intelligent Triage & Patient Monitoring System

---

## 1. Problem Statement

Rural and under-resourced clinics face:

- Overcrowding
- Limited medical staff
- Delayed patient prioritization
- Manual record keeping
- Late detection of patient deterioration

In many cases, patients with critical conditions wait too long because clinics lack structured triage systems.

This leads to:

- Preventable complications
- Increased mortality
- Staff burnout
- Inefficient resource allocation

---

## 2. Purpose of the Project

The purpose of BoraCare AI is to:

1. Provide a lightweight AI-powered triage system
2. Automatically prioritize patients based on symptom severity
3. Monitor patient vitals over time
4. Alert healthcare workers to worsening conditions

It aims to bring structured clinical decision support to small rural clinics using accessible technology.

---

## 3. Proposed Solution

BoraCare AI is a web-based system that:

1. Collects patient symptoms
2. Applies rule-based AI scoring
3. Assigns urgency levels — **Low**, **Moderate**, **Critical**
4. Displays a prioritized queue for healthcare workers
5. Logs patient vitals for ongoing monitoring

> The system **assists**, not replaces, medical professionals.

---

## 4. Target Users

- Rural clinics
- Small hospitals
- Community health centers
- NGOs operating mobile clinics
- Government health departments

---

## 5. Key Features

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| AI Triage Scoring | Symptom-based urgency scoring engine |
| Urgency Classification | Automated Low / Moderate / Critical labels |
| Patient Queue Dashboard | Prioritized view for healthcare workers |
| Vital Signs Logging | Track and log patient vitals over time |
| Historical Records | View past patient data and trends |

---

## 6. Innovation

While large hospitals use complex Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems, rural clinics often rely on manual processes.

BoraCare AI introduces:

- Low-cost intelligent triage
- Digital patient prioritization
- Early deterioration detection
- Data-driven clinical decision support

All designed for **low-resource environments**.

---

## 7. Social Impact

This system could:

1. Reduce patient waiting time for critical cases
2. Improve survival rates
3. Reduce staff workload
4. Standardize triage practices
5. Improve healthcare accessibility in underserved regions

> In regions like rural Kenya, structured triage systems are extremely limited, making this solution especially impactful.
